Lodging

We're mixing it up a little this year and giving everyone a sleeping space. That required us to move literally across the road to the Forest Trail townhomes. That's right, we have three duplex style townhomes.

If you're into preplanning, search for Forest Trails on Spearhead Drive. It's right at the infamous bus stop, and offers ski-in ski-out access by a forest trail (see what they did there?) that goes down to the Blackcomb village.

Transportation

More info will be posted here over the next few weeks but here's perhaps a major point: If you're planning on driving on non-standard days (meaning not Thur & Sunday) email Ben so he can connect you with other singles in your area... no, no that's not it. Email Ben so he can connect you with the other people that are coming late/leaving early.

Skiing

For the few that are new to this trip, we actually have a wide array of skiing levels in this group. Don't think you need to be a pro skier to have fun in Whistler. And even if you don't want to ski, there are other things. One year people snowshoed. And there's the whole olympic park! I bet you can bobsled. Or luge!

Anyway, for this skiers, lift tickets are now a criminally high $99 per day. For Washington residents, I suggest an Edge Card. Other past methods include trying to get someone leaving the slopes to give you their lift ticket. It didn't work very well. We just ended up watching Hunt for Red October back at the hotel.

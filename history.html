The Whistler Trip

A Critical Look Back

2006 – Whistler: An Introduction

In the early winter months of 2006 a large group of Seattle transplants, still exploring their new city, decided to take their first official group road trip to the unknown frontier of British Columbia. The waters we tested. Coincidentally, that same weekend saw the completion of the 2006 winter Olympics in… Italy? Soviet Russia? Somewhere. The important part here is that the Whistler village was a giant party in celebration of the official hand-off of the Olympics to Whistler for the 2010 games. It was a big deal. Someone like… Third Eye Blind was there. Or maybe Fall Out Boy? Whoever the kids listened to in 2006. 

We spent the trip establishing some key best practices for futures years. You probably need reservations for a 20+ seating for sushi dinner. Flexibility in sleeping arrangements is required. We are expert skiiers.

While 2006 isn’t remembered by all, this first foray into Canada set the stage for…
 

2007 – Whistler: Apologies to the Woodrun
2007 begins a three-year run of what is remembered in the literature as the "Whistler Renaissance." We found our groove, as well as the Woodrun. And with that, the three-day Whistler weekend as you know it today was born.

This weekend included such hits as midnight sledding on the slopes, Jeff Ma fighting some guy with a frying pan and Naked Will. We discovered sake-ritas, we hottubed and Sarah violated the giant Woodrun bear statue. Jeff also made a snow... rocketship.

In hindsight, we should count ourselves lucky that this wasn't our last legal entrance into Canada.
 

2008 – Whistler: Getting the Hang of it
Historically the least photographed Whistler trip, 2008 focused on repeating 2007's success (again at the poor, poor Woodrun) with more people. 2008 Saw a record number of non-skiers and proved that Whistler has entertainment to offer all. 2008 was your standard second in a trillogy: Nothing notable, but also fundamental to the learning experience. After all, it was followed by...

Whistler 2009: The Bus Stop
Where to begin? 2009 didn't necessarily have anything new going for it. And yet, when the time came, we had a trip that is remembered with such phrases as "We peaked as human beings," "That bar should have paid us to be there" and "We broke everything in the condo."

2009 had us singing in a bus stop, marching to the rhythm of Steve's whistle through the village, completely taking over a dance floor and then proceeding to follow Nash (dressed as Richard Simmons) through a workout routine. Joe in leather pants. Mouties threatening us with tasers. 2009 remains the gold standard for Whistler trips.

Whistler 2010: SLC
You'd think after 2009 we might be all like "Maybe we shouldn't fly too close to the sun" or "What price might we pay for man's hubris?" Whatever. Mike Nash got us a CASTLE in Salt Lake City and then everyone just bought tickets and went. We are immortal. Rules to not apply to us at this point.

We relocated the trip to a literal porn palace outside of Salt Lake City, put 30 people in it with room to spare, and had an amazing ski weekend at two resorts.

In the meantime we had a dance party (Theme: Onesies), flew through the air with the greatest of ease (into a room literally full of beanbags), played beer pong with Nashes uncle and watched the Oscars.
